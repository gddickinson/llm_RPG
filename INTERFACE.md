## Module Index

### engine/battle/ — Phase 17 tactical layer

- **`battle_data.py`** — loaders over `data/battles/*.json`: unit archetypes, formations, matchup RPS + terrain, fortifications, P17.6a grid-terrain cover (`terrain_cover`), P17.10 `ARMOUR` (classes + shield); all content, no hardcoded stats.
- **`battle_aoe.py`** — P17.12 area effects & battle magic (pure over the field): `tiles_in_radius` (Chebyshev cluster), `blast` (radius damage, fiercest at impact, armour-typed, optional structure hits → hit/killed), `fireball` (blast + ignite the cluster via `battle_fire`, flame ignores armour), `cast(spell,…)` (fireball/oil/blast). The session routes siege `blast_radius` splash + the `battle_mage` archetype's `_cast_spell` here.
- **`battle_armour.py`** — P17.10 armour, shields & damage types (pure) over `data/battles/armour.json`: `resist_mult`/`apply_resist` (an armour class turns some damage TYPES and is punched through by others — mail shrugs a slash but an arrow/pick pierces it, plate turns blade & point but a mace's blunt shock transfers), `shield_dc_bonus` (a FRONT-arc to-hit bonus, worth more vs ranged than melee), `weight_of`/`speed_penalty` (the armour+shield weight tax — built & tested, wiring into `Squad.speed` deferred to P17.10b). Every field optional; `battle_ai.attack` calls in.
- **`battle_unit.py`** — P17.2 grid pieces: `Soldier` (light token, +P17.4c `move_accum` speed budget, +P17.9 `reload_left` shot cooldown & `moved_last` move-and-shoot flag) + `Squad` (owns soldiers, ONE morale bar, order/formation/commander, `speed`; rout on morale threshold); round-trips to dict.
- **`battle_fire.py`** — P17.E4 fire & the battle surface layer: `ignite` (a connected oil pool all catches), `pour_oil` (boiling oil), and `tick(field, rng)` (run each round from the session) — fire burns the soldier on it, eats combustible terrain (forest/hedge → scorched), breaches a timber gate/wall, spreads to neighbours, guts out to scorched. `archer_fire`'s `fire_arrow` stat lights the struck tile via `attack`.
- **`battle_terrain.py`** — P17.E1/E2 terrain combat math (pure): P17.E1 elevation — `height_to_hit` (downhill +, uphill −, capped), `charge_dmg_mult` (downhill momentum), `height_reach` (a hill extends bow range/sight); P17.E2 obstacles — `move_cost` (wading a stream/bog costs extra move budget) and `anchored` (a flank/rear resting on impassable terrain takes no arc bonus); P17.E3 `has_los` (Bresenham line-walk — sight-blocking terrain hides a target, so no shooting through a treeline or wall). Wired into `battle_ai.attack`/`charge_attack`/`_position_mods` + the session's movement + LOS-gated in-reach test. Zero-cost on open, flat ground.
- **`battle_field.py`** — P17.2 battle grid: own terrain (P17.6a passable COVER terrains + `cover_at`), WALL/GATE as HP structures breaching to rubble lanes, soldier occupancy, squad/team registry; round-trips to dict for mid-battle saves; P17.6c capture-point objectives (`add_objective`/`team_counts_near`/`captured_team`); P17.E1 sparse `elevation` layer (`set_elevation`/`elevation_at`); P17.E2 obstacle terrains (moat/cliff/chasm in `BLOCKING`, stream/ditch/bog in the slow-`PASSABLE` set).
- **`battle_flow.py`** — P17.3 flow fields: multi-source BFS distance field per team (O(map)); soldiers step the gradient toward the enemy and through breaches.
- **`battle_ai.py`** — P17.3 group AI (ported colosseum brain): focus-fire target select, self-contained d20 strike, role movement (archers kite), squad morale/rout; P17.4c `step_toward` greedy push-into-contact when the centroid flow is blocked; P17.6b `nearest_struct` for siege engines homing on walls; P17.13 `charge_attack`/`_shove` (charging cavalry & beasts trample loose foot, shatter on braced spears); P17.11 `_position_mods` (flank/rear/gang-up/surround bonuses via `battle_facing`); P17.15 positional morale in `update_morale`/`attack` (flank/rear hits shake the squad, a boxed-in SHARE saps nerve while depth resists, a nearby rout CASCADES, and routed squads are run down at +4/×1.5); P17.9 ranged fidelity in `attack` — `ranged_reach` scales reach by `range_factor` (longbow > crossbow > base), loosing a `reload > 0` weapon arms `reload_left` (gate + tick-down in the session), and a shot the tick after moving takes `MOVE_SHOOT_PENALTY` unless the shooter `can_parthian`; P17.10 `attack` reads the defender's arc once → `battle_armour.shield_dc_bonus` on the DC and `apply_resist` on the damage (armour-vs-damage-type); P17.6e `cover_seek_step` (a foot archer in the open sidesteps to the best in-range, in-LOS cover).
- **`battle_orders.py`** — P17.5 the commander's verbs as behaviour: `advance_intent` maps an order (charge/hold/fall_back/move/focus_fire) to a soldier's out-of-reach intent; `is_focus` concentrates fire; objective-type scaffold. The session + `pick_target` read it.
- **`battle_session.py`** — P17.3 deterministic tick loop; `run_headless(max_ticks)` runs squads to a result (same shape as the resolver); P17.4c multi-step movement by unit `speed` (`MAX_STEPS` cap); P17.5 order-driven movement (`_order_move`: hold/retreat/goto/advance); P17.6b siege battering (rams hammer an adjacent wall to a breach before shooting) + P17.6d ranged bombardment (artillery lobs at a wall from `SIEGE_RANGE`); P17.6c capture-point victory (`_update_objectives`; `over`/`result` end on a seizure); P17.13 `_charge_melee` (charge/overrun momentum in melee); P17.19 `battle_doctrine.apply` per squad each tick; P17.20 `_flee` fires the Parthian shot (`ai.can_parthian`) and routed-but-alive squads keep fleeing every tick; P17.9 each tick snapshots start-positions → sets every soldier's `moved_last` (feeds the move-and-shoot penalty) and ticks `reload_left` down, gating a reloading shooter's shot and tracer (bombardment reloads too); P17.12 siege `blast_radius` splashes the ranks by the struck wall and a `battle_mage` routes to `_cast_spell` (an area burst via `battle_aoe`) instead of a single strike; P17.6e `_seek_cover` lets an in-range foot archer duck to cover before it looses; P17.7 `embodied` (a driven soldier's sid) is skipped by the tick's AI while `embody`/`unembody`/`embody_move`/`embody_attack`/`embodied_soldier` are the player's manual controls.
- **`battle_formation.py`** — P17.16/17 formations: `cohesion` (share of the squad facing the dominant way and clustered), `is_broken` (< 0.5), and per-formation effects — LINE shield-overlap `defense_bonus` (+2 front-arc with a right-mate), `speed_mult` (½ for LINE/RING), `steady` (LINE depth morale floor); LOOSE `incoming_ranged_mult` (½); the all-facing RING's `effective_arc` (every arc → front, the surround-counter), missile-vulnerability (×1.5) and `attack_penalty` (−2); the WEDGE's `wedge_charge_bonus` (+3 charge to-hit to breach, no defensive guard — P17.18); `check_break` latches the one-time morale shock. Wired into `battle_ai.attack`/`_position_mods`/`update_morale` and `battle_session._steps`. P17.17 BRACE is `battle_ai._is_braced` (a `braced` flag or `order=="hold"`), gating `charge_attack`'s anti-cavalry hedge. P17.18 hammer-and-anvil is `battle_ai._is_pinned` (a different-squad frontal enemy) → −6 rout-shock on a flank/rear hit in `attack`.
- **`battle_doctrine.py`** — P17.19 commander AI instincts, run each tick from the session: `should_brace`/`incoming_charger` (a brace-capable squad sets to receive when an enemy charger closes within 6), `should_commit`/`local_advantage` (a holding reserve piles into a nearby fight it already wins locally), `apply` writes the stance back. Deterministic; uses the P17.17 brace + P17.18 reserve concepts.
- **`battle_facing.py`** — P17.11 pure facing/arc geometry: 8-dir `DIRS`, `face_toward`, `arc` → front/flank/rear; feeds the flank/rear to-hit & damage bonuses in `battle_ai`.
- **`battle_scenario.py`** — P17.4 staged set-pieces from `data/battles/scenarios.json`; `build_field(id)` expands one into a tickable `BattleField` (shared by the screen and the tests).
- **`battle_resolve.py`** — P17.1 headless Lanchester auto-resolver: `Army`/`Unit`/`Fort` + seeded `resolve(attacker, defender, terrain, is_siege, seed)`; defence-reduced melee, RPS+speed-softened ranged, cavalry charge, anti-cavalry spears, wall-gated siege with breaches. Deterministic; doubles as the off-screen faction-battle resolver.

### engine/ — Core game logic

- **`game_engine.py`** — `GameEngine`; the thin orchestrator: state, start/end, delegates (438 lines).
- **`netplay.py`** — M.4a networked-play keystone: the transport-free client<->world contract. `Intent` (whitelisted move/attack/say/wait verb naming the acting hero; JSON round-trip) + `GameServer` owning the engine — `join`/`leave` seat heroes in the roster+world, `submit` applies one intent through the real player-action route acting-as that hero WITHOUT ticking the world, `tick` is the server's own world clock, `snapshot` is a JSON view (heroes + nearby NPC bodies). The socket transport (M.4b) is a thin layer over this.
- **`net_server.py`** — M.4b message layer & dispatch (transport-free): newline-JSON framing (`encode`/`FrameDecoder`), the JOIN/INTENT/LEAVE/POLL → WELCOME/RESULT/SNAPSHOT/ERROR protocol, and `NetServer` owning one `GameServer` — connection registry, `on_message` dispatch that binds each client's intents to the hero it JOINED as (anti-spoof), `tick_and_broadcast` for the shared clock. The wire pumps this.
- **`net_socket.py`** — M.4b the real TCP wire over `net_server`: `NetHost` (threaded `ThreadingTCPServer` + optional background ticker that advances + broadcasts snapshots) and `NetClient` (connect/join/intent/poll/leave, caches latest snapshot). Stdlib sockets only; opt-in — the single-process game never imports it.
- **`agent_controller.py`** — M.2 `AgentController` playing a hero through the real player-action API via `acting_as`; `drive_agents(engine)` runs agent roster heroes each turn (advance_turn re-entrancy-guarded). M.2b priority policy: survive (heal-potion/Heal-spell/flee under HP), don't fight swarmed, focus-fire + ranged, grab loot, else wander — LLM-free. Bug-fix 2026-07-12: `_can_shoot` requires matching AMMO (thrown excepted), so an empty-quivered agent closes to melee instead of dry-firing a bow forever in place. M.5 the LIVING away-hero: beyond combat it chats (`talk`), takes & pursues quests (`accept_quest`/`_quest_goal`), recruits (`recruit`), and explores toward class-flavoured named places (`_named_goal`/`_CLASS_DRAW`) — biased by the player's `disposition` setting (balanced/valiant/cautious/sociable/explorer/greedy) — writing `[Away]` deed beats and its `agent_goal` into the record for the player to review. Freeze-proofing 2026-07-12b (delegated to `agent_nav`): every move is walkability-checked so a hero pinned against a wall SIDESTEPS (or, cornered, turns and fights) instead of spinning a blocked flee; inside a building it makes for the door and steps back out (`_zone_plan`/`exit_building`) rather than chasing unreachable overworld goals; and `_colocated` gates perception to the hero's own grid so it never locks onto — and "shoots" forever at — a phantom foe standing in overworld-space seen through an interior wall. Loop-proofing (same day): `_nearest_loot` skips plain-string BODY MARKERS (a corpse isn't loot) and returns nothing when the pack is FULL (so the hero doesn't stand on an uncarryable item pumping pickup forever), the agent skirts buildings on the overworld (no doorway enter/exit shuffle), `flee_step`/`safe_step` carry an anti-oscillation `recent` trail, `_social_plan` stops re-approaching an already-greeted friend, and rule 2b RETREATS from a closing 3+ pack (a lair/warband) before it boxes the hero in and kills it over and over — together ending the freeze/oscillate/death-loop cluster George caught on the live watch. M.8a recovery (2026-07-12d, decide step 3b): a SAFE hero below `REST_HP` tops up with a potion/Heal, or — badly hurt on the overworld out of quick heals — makes CAMP (the `rest` verb → `engine.rest.sleep`), gated on `agent_sense._provisioned` so an unprovisioned doze doesn't loop every night; adventurers never rest. Goal/disposition helpers live in `agent_goals`.
- **`anim.py`** — P33.6c/P34 animation triggers (engine-side, pygame-free): `emote`/`stance`/`express`/`bubble`/`face`/`look`/`interact` set the `metadata` keys `body_renderer` reads; `update_look` (per-turn gaze at the nearest actor), `update_swim` (swim stance on deep water), and P34.4 `update_idle_life(engine, rng=None)` — an idle non-hostile character occasionally plays a role/needs-weighted ambient FIDGET (from the P34.10 library) or STARTLES at a nearby hostile (alert bubble + faces it); cheap, cosmetic, injectable rng.
- **`agent_nav.py`** — the away-agent's movement-safety layer (2026-07-12b, split from `agent_controller` to hold the line): pure functions over (engine, char) — `walkable` (zone-aware: the interior/dungeon floor when inside one, else the overworld with its wall-guard AND buildings treated as solid so the agent skirts them rather than shuffling in a doorway), `flee_step` (a walkable escape that opens distance, anti-backtrack via an `avoid` trail, None when cornered), `safe_step` (step toward a goal but only onto a reachable tile, `avoid`-penalised so it never oscillates between two tiles — routes around walls instead of freezing), `zone_roam` (a random walkable in-zone tile). The guarantee that a driven hero never freezes OR loops.
- **`agent_sense.py`** — the away-agent's stateless sensing/eligibility predicates (2026-07-12b, split to hold the line): `_is_hostile`, `_colocated` (same-grid check — no phantom foes across coordinate spaces), `_healing_item`, `_knows_heal`, `_can_shoot` (needs matching ammo), `_provisioned` (enough food for a REAL camp — the gate that stops M.8a's rest from looping), `_attack_spell` (M.8c: the most mana-efficient reachable damage spell a caster knows — so it fights with magic), `_gatherable` (M.8d: the hero's own tile is a workable node or a rich forest/swamp forage — with carry room — so it stops to gather raws + forest BREAD, which feeds the M.8a camp), `_learn_item`/`_can_pray` (M.8e: a teaching tome/manual worth studying, and standing at a shrine unprayed today), `_can_stash`/`_surplus_items`/`_claim_target` (M.8f: a full pack + a furnished home to shelve loot in, and an affordable derelict to claim). M.10a needs self-care: `_thirsty`/`_hungry`/`_tired` (act before it's dire), `_drink_item`/`_food_item` (a carried drink/edible), `_adjacent_water`/`_water_toward` (drink from — or make for — the river).
- **`agent_goals.py`** — the away-agent's exploration goals & disposition (M.8a split): `CLASS_DRAW` (where each calling roams), `named_goal(ctrl,…)` (nearest unvisited class-flavoured place), `pick_goal(ctrl,…)` (potter home / strike out), `disposition(char)`.
- **`agent_exec.py`** — the away-agent's action execution (split from `agent_controller` to hold the line): `execute(ctrl, engine, char, plan)` turns a decided plan into real player actions, acting AS the driven character (the attack/shoot/cast/loot/forage/study/pray/rest/talk/quest/trade/recruit/exit/move dispatch).
- **`agent_trade.py`** — the away-agent's economy (M.8b): `wants_to_trade`/`do_trade` — standing by a merchant, the hero sells its JUNK loot and buys the essentials it's short of (a potion when it carries none → feeds M.8a; ammo when it can't fire its bow), through `ShopManager.buy_for`/`sell_for`. Loot → gold → readiness.
- **`away_digest.py`** — M.9a the "While You Were Away" return digest: `snapshot(engine, char)` (stamped by `set_away(True)` — turn/day/level/gold/party + a memory index) and `build_digest(engine, char)` → a `(title, lines)` overlay tallying the deltas (days/levels/purse/new companions) + the `[Away]` deed beats since, one-shot (consumes the snapshot). The GUI hand-back pops it as a menu overlay.
- **`away_mode.py`** (in `ui/`) — M.3 absent-player heartbeat: `heartbeat(gui)` ticks the world while the active hero's human is away (so the driving agent keeps it alive), at a watcher-chosen cadence (M.9b `SPEEDS` paused/slow/normal/fast/blitz, `cycle_speed`/`single_step`/`handle_speed_key` — `[-/+]` speed, `[.]` step) + the `banner_text` AUTOPLAY indicator (shows the speed) + M.9c `spectator_lines(engine)` (the away-hero's aim/bearing/standing/band for the spectator card, `hud.draw_spectator_panel`). Bug-fix 2026-07-12d `hands_back(engine, event)`: only a hero-DIRECTING key (move/attack/act) returns control to the human — an OBSERVE key (settings `,`, a journal I/Q/O/J/U/Y, the map, help, save/load, Esc) does NOT, so opening the settings overlay to confirm autoplay no longer silently switched it off (the "autoplay doesn't seem to work" bug).
- **`player_roster.py`** — M.1 `PlayerRoster` (`engine.roster`) + `PlayerController` (human/agent): the roster of controllable characters over `engine.player`; add/`set_active`/`controller_for`, survives save/load via `metadata['controller']`. M.1b: non-active heroes live in the world (placed in `npc_manager` + on the map, flagged `player_char`, rendered as heroes, skipped by NPC AI); `rehydrate` rebuilds the roster after a load. M.3: `set_away`/`is_away`/`away_characters` — an away human's hero is driven by the agent (`drive_agents` includes away humans) until they return. The multiplayer/agent keystone.
- **`engine_setup.py`** — `build_subsystems(engine)`: every gameplay system constructed in dependency order (P14.1 split from __init__).
- **`turn_pipeline.py`** — `run_turn(engine)`: the per-minute pipeline — needs, encounters, companions, conflicts, surfaces, floods, hazards, dying, law, pets — plus the nightly stack; block order is load-bearing (P14.1 split from advance_turn).
- **`demo_setup.py`** — `initialize_demo_world(engine, spec, world_kind)`, `create_default_player(spec)`. P18.5 `world_kind="castle"` plants the `castle_region` realm and spawns the player at the gatehouse instead of the default Oakvale world.
- **`activities.py`** — LIVING_WORLD A1/A2 the per-turn ACTIVITY system over `data/activities.json` (George: make NPCs purposeful, not random). `ActivitySystem.perform(npc, activity)` — when a scheduled NPC has REACHED its work location, it PERFORMS the activity (a fitting `ui/char_clips` animation via `engine/anim` + expression + a sparse `[Town]` beat) instead of loitering: the clip comes from the ACTIVITY (pray→kneel, play→dance, eat/drink) and, for `work`, is refined by the NPC's PROFESSION (`profession_of` = its `home_location` building kind → `building_types.profession_of_kind` → smith→hammer, cook→stir…), else its class (`class_work`). `is_perform` gates work/pray/play/eat/drink. A2 `patrol_step` walks a guard's BEAT — the settlement GATES (else a ring) via BFS `squad_tactics.path_step`, sticky. A3 `_goto_worksite`/`_worksite_for` fan workers out to a stable personal tile at the workplace (no stacking) + `_draw_crowd` turns nearby folk to face a performing bard; A4 `_work_yield` stocks a GATHERER's raw (`production.primary_raw`) into the settlement store (crafters stay cosmetic — the nightly loop crafts). Nearby-only, heuristic, no per-tick LLM; state on metadata. `action_router._handle_move` dispatches here on arrival; `characters/schedules.py` archetypes (A5) give the previously-scheduleless classes a day-plan. Validated by `check_activities` + `tests/test_activities.py`.
- **`action_router.py`** — Routes NPC actions to specialized handlers. LOITER (George): a scheduled NPC that has REACHED its location keyword (most schedule activities map to `("move", <location>)` via `schedules.activity_to_action`) used to freeze on one tile — `_interpret_direction` returns (0,0) when already at the target. Now `_handle_move` detects arrival (within `LOITER_RADIUS`=3 of the resolved location) and `_loiter_step` STROLLS the NPC toward a wander point in the area (fresh point on arrival; any-neighbour fallback when blocked; no backtrack; `LOITER_PAUSE_CHANCE`=0.15), so idle towns stay in continuous ambient motion instead of NPCs converging and standing. Compass/toward-target moves are untouched.
- **`combat_system.py`** — Player vs NPC vs NPC combat, damage, defeat, loot, faction rep on kill.
- **`terrain_combat.py`** — P35.3 terrain & cover combat rules (pure): `cover_ac` (FOREST/RUBBLE you stand in or a BUILDING/wall you hug = harder to hit, more vs ranged), `footing_penalty` (WATER/MARSH hampers a melee swing), `high_ground` (an archer beside a MOUNTAIN gets a +2 edge), `cover_score` (for the AI). Applied in `combat_system._resolve`; the pack AI's `_surround_tile` favours cover tiles.
- **`combat_math.py`** — pure combat math split out of `combat_system` to hold the 500-line line: `damage_type_modifier` (silver vs troll/monster, holy vs the wicked, fire denies a troll its regen) which also folds in T4.4 `skill_combat.beast_damage_bonus` (a hunter's flat edge vs beasts).
- **`stealth.py`** — GAP.3 stealth & sneak attacks (George's "what's missing" — the STEALTH skill was vestigial). ALL gated on the opt-in CRAWL stance (`.`-key `_move_mode=="crawl"`), so default play + every existing pursuit/aggression/combat test is untouched. `is_sneaking`; `detection_radius(engine, target)` (a crawling player is noticed within `BASE_DETECT`=7 minus `evasion_rating` — DEX mod + level + rogue calling + dark, floored at 1); `detects`/`evades` (a sneaking, un-noticed, out-of-detection, no-LOS player is HIDDEN — `pursuit.update`/`aggression.update` skip an evaded target, so you creep up or slip past); `is_unaware`/`note_engaged`/`forget`/`mark_combat_awareness` (awareness on `npc.metadata["noticed_player"]` — set when blows are exchanged or a pursuer engages, cleared when it loses you past `CHASE_RADIUS`); `sneak_power`/`apply_sneak_bonus` (+60%..+250% by DEX/level/rogue) + `sneak_attack`. Wired into `combat_system._resolve` (captures unaware BEFORE marking the fight joined — any attack alerts, a hit on the caught-unaware foe is a SNEAK ATTACK beat, which unlocks the codex entry), `pursuit`/`aggression` (the evasion gate), and a crawl hint-bar cue.
- **`skill_combat.py`** — T4.4 skills feed combat power (pure, data-driven over each skill's optional `combat` block in `data/skills.json`): `weapon_damage_bonus` (smithing hones your blade), `armor_ac_bonus` (smithing sound armour + agility dodge), `beast_damage_bonus(attacker,target)` (hunting vs a monster/animal), `combat_summary` (character-sheet lines). Each tie is `+1 per N skill levels`; wired into `effects.effective_ac`/`effective_weapon_damage_bonus` + `combat_math`. Validated by `items/validate_world.check_skill_combat` + `tests/test_skill_combat.py`.
- **`economy_system.py`** — Buy/sell/trade/give between characters.
- **`dialog_system.py`** — Player↔NPC dialog flow (routes through dialog_protocol for LLM providers).
- **`conversation.py`** — PUX.6 the conversation quick-pick menu: `menu(engine, npc)` lists an NPC's quests (accept/turn-in), Trade (`is_merchant`), askable topics, and unlockable secrets as numbered options the dialog screen dispatches.

- **`creature_anim.py`** — #9b ANIMATED creatures (George: "they aren't animated"): the Quaternius GLBs ship RIGGED with Walk/Idle/Gallop/Attack/Eating/Death clips + a skin, so this SKINS the mesh (joint world matrices × inverse-bind × per-vertex weights, `_frame_meshes`/`_world_mats`/`_sample` with nlerp rotations) at `NFRAMES`=8 phases of a clip and bakes each to a cached iso sprite (`_frame_sprite`, lru-cached per species/clip/frame/size). `_action_and_phase(char)` reads the shared `_anim` state → (walk from `move_phase` / idle from `idle_phase` / attack from `atk_t` / hurt / dead), `clip_for` picks the species' best clip for the action (`_CLIP_PREF`), and `animated_sprite(char,size,face_east)` returns the current frame (h-flipped east) — a beast STRIDES/breathes/strikes instead of standing frozen. Cached warm calls are ~µs; the first sighting bakes a clip's frames (~50 ms each, shared across all instances of the species). Graceful fallback to `creature_glb.sprite_for_char`. Consumed by `creature_render._draw_model` (top-down) + `iso_actors.beast_sprite` (iso). Validated by `tests/test_creature_anim.py`.
- **`char_fx.py`** — P34.19 physical-effect overlays (pure/thin): deterministic rising FLAMES while `_fx_fire` and a bluish sheen + running DRIPS while `_fx_wet`, drawn over a character by `body_renderer`/`creature_render`. The thrown-through-air tumble (`launched`) and the on-fire panic (`flail`) are pose clips; `engine.anim.launch`/`update_fx` set + decay the state (fire surface, water, `tactics.shove`).
- **`char_injury.py`** — P34.17 injury & state pose modifiers (pure): `injury_state` reads wounds/dying off `metadata`; `apply_limp` (leg wound → a hitching, dragging gait), `apply_arm` (arm wound → the arm hangs limp), and a DOWNED override (dying/unconscious/KO → the `lie` pose). `body_renderer` applies them after the clip.
- **`char_flow.py`** — P34.5 hair / cloak / weapon-trail flow (pure physics + thin draw): a body-LOCAL verlet CHAIN (`init_chain`/`step_chain`) for a trailing ponytail (all) + a billowing cloak (`CLOAK_CLASSES`), and a fading weapon-swing TRAIL (`push_trail`). `body_renderer` draws the cloak+hair behind the body, the trail in front; camera-stable like the P34.3 springs.
- **`char_pose3d.py`** — P34.14/15 CONTINUOUS-FACING pose (pure): every joint in body coords (u across / w fore-aft depth / y up) PROJECTED by a facing angle θ (`pose3d`; `pose3d_mocap` feeds a baked MOCAP clip's fore-aft into the depth so real stride plays at any facing; `spine_for` mood curve) — `screen_x=cx+(u·cosθ+w·sinθ)·H`), a drop-in for `char_pose.build_pose` (gait/bob/attack) that renders ANY heading; `cam_depth` per joint drives the near/far limb sort, `facing_from_delta` maps a move to an angle. `body_renderer` eases a `face_cur` angle toward the heading and renders through this — the cast turns to face any of 360°.
- **`char_style.py`** — P34.11 per-character MOTION STYLE (pure): `gait_of` (id-seeded stride/bob/arm/cadence multipliers), `attack_style` (overhead/thrust/slash — by weapon then id) and `cast_style` (staff-slam/point/two-hand). `body_renderer` feeds these to `char_pose.build_pose` (gait + attack_style), the mocap cadence, and the cast-clip choice, so the cast walks, fights and casts as individuals.
- **`char_clips_util.py`** — P34.10 shared clip primitives (pure): `_arc`/`_ease`/`_fdir`/`_move`/`_feet_pivot`/`_centroid`/`_rotate` (whole-body spin) + `_swing`, the vocabulary both clip modules build on (re-exports `scale_pose`).
- **`char_clips_fun.py`** — P34.13 comedy & dance clips (pure): jig, can-can kick, moonwalk, robot, flex, taunt, wiggle, disco, air-guitar, two-hand facepalm. Exports `ACTIONS_FUN`/`CLIPS_FUN`, merged into `char_clips` at import; the `;` key (`input_actions.perform_emote`) plays a random one.
- **`char_clips_more.py`** — P34.10 the expanded clip library (pure): +21 acrobatics & daily-life transforms — flip/somersault/cartwheel/roll/twirl, lunge/crawl/crouch, eat/drink/rest/lie/stretch/yawn/clap/laugh/shrug/ponder/salute/beckon/facepalm. Exports `ACTIONS_MORE`/`CLIPS_MORE`, which `char_clips` merges into its registry at import (kept separate to hold the 500-line line).
- **`char_secondary.py`** — P34.3 secondary motion (pure): `spring2` (critically-damped 2D spring the head/weapon lag behind on), `look_dir` (cone-clamped look direction toward a tile), `ease2`. `body_renderer` integrates these over a persistent `metadata['_anim']['_sec']` substrate — head follow-through, a whippy weapon tip, and a look-at that shifts the head + leads the pupils. `engine/anim.update_look` sets the per-turn gaze target (nearest actor).
- **`char_face.py`** — P34.2 facial expression + blink + emote bubbles (pure): 8 `EXPRESSIONS` ({brow tilt, mouth curve, eye mode} → neutral/happy/laughing/angry/sad/scared/surprised/hurt), an `EMOTE_EXPR` map (an action → a fleeting face), `expr_for(char)` (reads `metadata['_expr']`), a jittered `blink_step(anim,dt,seed)` state machine, and the `BUBBLES` list. `body_parts.draw_face`/`draw_bubble` render it; `body_renderer` picks the expression from the current action else the mood.
- **`char_clips.py`** — P33.6b the animation CLIP library (pure, headless-testable): an `ACTIONS` registry (one-shot vs held-loop + durations) + 13 procedural pose transforms — `jump`/`leap`/`sit`/`sleep`/`bow`/`stoop`/`wave`/`guard`/`hurt`/`cast`/`dance`/`cheer`/`run` — each mutating a COPY of the `char_pose` rest skeleton for a phase; `apply(action,pose,phase,H,facing)` dispatches. `body_renderer._update_action` is the state machine that picks the current action (a one-shot emote, an attack, a held `_stance`, walk/run, or idle) and `draw_body` applies the clip. Requested via `char_motion.emote`/`set_stance`/`face_toward`; `combat_system` already faces the attacker + recoils the struck body.
- **`body_parts.py`** — P33.4b the thin pygame drawing of a posed character: `draw_legs`/`draw_torso`/`draw_arms` (circle-capped limbs + a filled torso + belt), `draw_head` (haired head + a FRONT two-eye face or a SIDE nose+one-eye profile, race ears/tusks), `draw_shield`, and `draw_weapon` (sword/axe/mace/spear/staff/dagger/bow aligned to the forearm). Consumed by `body_renderer`.
- **`combat_effects.py`** — Damage popups, hit flashes, death particles (effects live in world tile coords). P41.12 `draw_with(target, view, to_screen, ts)` is the projection-agnostic core — `draw(cam_x, cam_y, ts)` (top-down) builds a grid `to_screen` closure over it, and the iso renderer passes an `IsoProjection` closure — so combat reads in BOTH views from one effect implementation.
- **`light_palette.py`** — P15.4 pure light/weather colour: `light_color(kind)` (coloured light sources — forge orange, wisp blue-green, …) and `sky_tint(hour, conjunction, weather, season)` (green aurora on clear conjunction nights, cool winter chill while snowing / on winter nights, eased by `animation.ambient_darkness`). Used by `lighting.py`.
- **`lighting.py`** — Night darkness + torch/window light punches (weather-scaled); P15.4 marsh-wisp blue-green light + `_apply_sky_tint` (aurora / winter wash) via `light_palette`. Night torch-pool widened per George (2026-07-15): player radius 7.5 tiles, companion 5.0, a gentle `_radial_light` ^1.5 falloff (more of the disc reads lit), and a lifted `TOD_DARKNESS` night floor (148) so the ground beyond the pool stays dim-but-visible.
- **`weather_overlay.py`** — Rain/snow/fog particle overlays.
- **`sound.py`** — Procedural SFX (numpy-synthesized) via event observer + weather ambience loops.
- **`music_synth.py`** — GAP.1 procedural MUSIC synthesis (pure numpy, no assets, headless-testable): modal/pentatonic `SCALES`, `midi_to_freq`, and layer synths — `pad` (a sustained chord bed that breathes), `arpeggio` (a seeded melodic random-walk over the scale), `bassline` (a low root/fifth pulse); `render_mood(params, seed)` mixes the enabled layers into ONE loopable float buffer, seamless at the loop point (`_loop_blend` continues the pad past the loop + blends; event layers taper to silence). Knows nothing about pygame.
- **`music.py`** — GAP.1 the ADAPTIVE score over `music_synth` + `data/music.json`: `MusicManager` renders each mood to a loopable Sound on first use and CROSSFADES between moods as the game state changes (two reserved mixer channels, `FADE_DUR`). `select_mood(...)` is the pure policy (combat > tension > dungeon > town > night > explore) and `read_state(engine)` extracts the cheap state it needs (nearest colocated hostile distance, in-dungeon/town, night); `update_mood(engine, dt)` re-picks on a throttle and honours the "music" setting live. Constructed in `gui`, ticked in the main loop; degrades silently headless.
- **`animation.py`** — P15.2 pure, headless-testable animation math behind the pixels (the `battle_camera` move for the main renderer): `clamp`/`lerp`/`smoothstep`/`lerp_color` interpolation vocabulary, `frame_index` (two-frame animation clock), `surface_fill` (P10.3 surface palette as data — fire flicker / electrified crackle / water shimmer, oil+blood inert; used by `renderer`), `ambient_darkness(hour)` (eased per-minute day↔night curve; used by `lighting`).
- **`battle_camera.py`** — P17.4 pure zoom/pan/LOD math for the battle screen (tile_size 8/16/32/48, float camera, world↔screen, blob_mode < 16px) + P17.4b unit-type glyph geometry (`category_shape`/`marker_points`); unit-tested headless.
- **`battle_screen.py`** — P17.4 the zoomable Battle Testbed view: a standalone pygame loop (no engine) that watches a `BattleSession` tick a scenario — terrain, soldiers-or-blobs by LOD, HUD, play/pause/step/reset; reachable from the start menu; P17.5 command overlay (TAB/click select an allied squad, C/H/F/G/M issue orders); P17.7 role-swap (`E` drops into the selected squad's lead — `_toggle_embody`/`_embodied_key` — WASD drives it, F strikes, camera locks via `center_on`, ESC releases).

## Engine subsystems wired to the game loop

Each turn (after a player move / dialog / action):
1. `world.advance_time(1)` — calendar minute passes
2. `quest_manager.on_turn_advanced()` — SURVIVE objectives tick
3. NPC needs tick — hunger / fatigue grow
4. `encounter_manager.maybe_spawn()` — chance of wilderness monster
5. `companion_manager.update()` — party members follow / fight
6. Every N turns: NPC actions (LLM or heuristic-based, including schedules)

## Adding new content — quick recipes

- **New item**: add an entry to the matching `data/items/*.json` file (only non-default fields needed).
- **New campaign pack**: drop a module JSON (monsters/items/spawns/quests/beats, see `engine/dm_modules.py` docstring) into `data/module_packs/`; use `"anchor": "wilderness"` instead of positions; run the validator.
- **New recipe**: add an entry to `data/recipes.json`.
- **New quest**: add an entry to `data/quests.json`; post to a board via `default_boards()` if appropriate.
- **New named NPC**: add an entry to the matching `data/npcs/*.json` file.
- **New NPC class**: extend `CharacterClass` in `characters/character_types.py`; add schedule in `characters/schedules.py`; add to `CLASS_TO_FACTION` in `characters/factions.py`.
- **New action**: add handler in `engine/action_router.py`.
- **New terrain**: extend `TerrainType` + add sprite in `ui/sprite_loader.py`.
- **New monster**: add an entry to `data/monsters.json` (`encounter_weight` for wilderness, `"dungeon": true` for dungeons).
- **New LLM provider**: subclass `LLMProvider` in `llm/providers/`, register in `llm/providers/__init__.py`.

## File size policy

All source files stay **under 500 lines** (per global coding preferences). Split modules before exceeding.
